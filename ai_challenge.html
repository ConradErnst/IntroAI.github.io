1. Overview

The development of Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) faces several significant challenges across technical, ethical, and conceptual domains. Current AI capabilities, while impressive, need to overcome various hurdles to achieve more advanced autonomous systems.

2. Common Sense and Reasoning

  • Transformer-based Reasoning: Models like GPT use attention mechanisms for contextual processing.
  • Neural-symbolic Integration: Combines neural networks with symbolic reasoning systems.
  • Probabilistic Reasoning: Uses Bayesian networks and probabilistic programming.

3. Planning and Execution

3.1 Traditional Planning Systems

  • STRIPS Planning:
    • State representation through predicates
    • Action definitions with preconditions and effects
    • Goal-directed search through state space
  • Hierarchical Task Networks (HTN):
    • Decomposition of complex tasks into subtasks
    • Domain-specific planning strategies
    • Efficient handling of real-world scenarios

3.2 Modern Planning Approaches

  • Monte Carlo Tree Search:
    • Probabilistic planning for large state spaces
    • Balancing exploration and exploitation
    • Application in game playing and decision making
  • Chain of Thought Planning:
    • Step-by-step reasoning processes
    • Integration with language models
    • Enhanced problem-solving capabilities
